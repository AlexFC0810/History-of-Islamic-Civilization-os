# Claims To Verify

This ledger protects the integrity of the project. Strong claims should not be treated as canon until researched, sourced, and graded.

## Evidence Grades

- **A:** Strong primary-source or widely accepted specialist consensus.
- **B:** Strong secondary-source support; needs primary-source confirmation.
- **C:** Plausible but contested; requires careful wording.
- **D:** Useful hypothesis, not yet public-ready.

## Priority Claims

### Animal Welfare Timeline
Claim: Islamic civilization developed enforceable animal welfare norms centuries before modern European animal welfare legislation.

Research needs:
- Primary Islamic sources on animal treatment.
- Fiqh/hisba examples of enforcement.
- Waqf examples for animals.
- European animal welfare law timeline, especially Martin's Act 1822.

Public wording constraint:
Avoid overclaiming "first ever" unless proven. Prefer: "centuries before modern European animal welfare law."

### No Compulsion In Religion
Claim: The Qur'an explicitly rejects compulsion in religion and this matters for understanding Islamic spread.

Research needs:
- Qur'an 2:256 context.
- Historical patterns of conversion.
- Regional case studies: Persia, North Africa, Al-Andalus, Central Asia, Southeast Asia, Mongols.

Public wording constraint:
Avoid simplistic "Islam never spread by conquest" wording. Better: "Conquest and conversion are not the same thing; Islamic sources explicitly reject forced conversion."

### Aisha Chronology
Claim: There are competing chronologies regarding Aisha's age; Asma-based chronology places Aisha older than the common polemical claim.

Research needs:
- Hadith corpus references.
- Asma chronology sources.
- Classical and modern scholarship.
- Methodological strengths/weaknesses.

Public wording constraint:
Do not present contested reconstruction as settled fact. Say: "The polemical version is not the only chronology; serious historical triangulation complicates the claim."
