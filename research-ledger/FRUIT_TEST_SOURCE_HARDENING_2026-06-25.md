# Fruit Test Source Hardening Ledger — 2026-06-25

## Purpose

This ledger tracks claims from the Fruit Test, Load-Bearing Questions, and Islam as Protection conversations that are powerful but must be source-hardened before public use.

The standard:

> Source hardening before virality.

Every major public-facing claim should move through:

1. claim;
2. source;
3. evidence grade;
4. caveat or counterclaim;
5. safe wording;
6. unsafe wording;
7. content angle.

## Source Stack

Use more than one civilizational lens.

1. **Primary Islamic sources:** Qur'an, rigorously graded hadith, sīrah, legal texts, treaties, letters.
2. **Muslim civilizational sources:** al-Tabari, al-Baladhuri, Ibn Khaldun, al-Biruni, Ibn Battuta, Ottoman records, waqf documents, Persian/Arabic chronicles.
3. **Local non-Western histories:** Indonesian, Malay, Indian, Persian, African, Turkish, Balkan, and Arab sources.
4. **Western academic sources:** useful but not sovereign.
5. **Global data sources:** WHO, UNODC, OECD, World Bank, Pew, UNICEF, UNESCO, Gallup, World Values Survey, Lancet, peer-reviewed journals.

The synthesis should not be "Western academia says." The synthesis should be:

> What survives triangulation across primary sources, civilizational memory, rival sources, and hard data?

## Claim Queue

### Claim 1: Islamic hospitals developed teaching-hospital features

**Claim:** Medieval Islamic hospitals were not merely places of care; some helped develop medical education, clinical training, records, pharmacy, and professionalized medicine.

**Status:** Needs source hardening.

**Evidence targets:**

- bimaristan scholarship;
- al-Mansuri hospital;
- al-Nuri hospital;
- Ahmad ibn Tulun hospital;
- Ottoman hospital records;
- teaching rounds / clinical instruction;
- physician licensing and medical manuals;
- pharmacy and medical record practices.

**Safe wording:**

> Medieval Islamic civilization helped develop sophisticated hospitals that combined care, teaching, medical learning, and charitable funding in ways that influenced later medical institutions.

**Unsafe wording:**

> Muslims invented hospitals.

**Content angle:**

> A civilization that hates knowledge does not build hospitals, libraries, observatories, and universities.

## Public Claim Standard

Before publishing, every claim should be labeled:

- **Sourced and safe** — ready for content;
- **Directionally true, needs caveat** — use only with careful wording;
- **High-potential, not verified** — research only;
- **Do not use** — too broad or unsupported.
