# Load-Bearing Questions Vault

## Purpose

This vault preserves the question strategy for responding to anti-Islam smears, shallow historical slogans, and propaganda frames without getting trapped in endless reactive debate.

A **load-bearing question** is compressed truth with a question mark.

It does not merely ask for information. It forces a slogan to carry the weight of reality.

## Core Doctrine

Do not debate every claim line-by-line when the claim is a vague slogan.

Instead:

1. translate the smear into a precise claim;
2. identify the category error;
3. find the largest counterexample;
4. ask the question in cold-friendly language;
5. concede the true part;
6. reject the propaganda overreach.

The posture is:

> Tell the truth. Refuse the smear.

## Cold-Friendly Standard

A line is not top-tier unless a cold audience can understand it without prior context.

### S-tier

- self-contained;
- concrete;
- memorable;
- morally serious;
- truthful under pressure;
- understandable by a stranger in one pass.

### A-tier

Strong, but needs one sentence of setup or a source card.

### B-tier

True, but too academic, too clever, too vague, or too fragile for mass use.

### C-tier

Correct but not contagious.

## Evaluation Rubric

Score every line on:

1. **Truth under pressure** — can a knowledgeable opponent easily nitpick it?
2. **Cold friendliness** — can a stranger understand it instantly?
3. **Compression** — can ordinary people repeat it?
4. **Moral force** — does it hit something deeper than trivia?
5. **Counterexample weight** — does it carry a massive fact?
6. **Memetic sharpness** — will it travel?

The goal is not cleverness. The goal is reality made portable.

## Blade Selection

Different smears need different blades.

- **Counterexample blade:** use when the smear is historically overbroad.
- **Moral inversion blade:** use when the smear assumes modernity is morally neutral.
- **Demographic contradiction blade:** use when the smear confuses Islam with ethnicity.
- **Definition trap blade:** use when the smear hides behind vague words.
- **Consistency test blade:** use when the smear applies one standard to Muslims and another to everyone else.
- **Civilizational contrast blade:** use when the smear ignores visible historical fruit.

## Master Prompt

Use this prompt to generate new entries:

> Take the smear: "[insert claim]." Build a load-bearing question set that is truthful under pressure, cold-friendly, memorable, and strong enough to force the other side to clarify. Avoid academic language. Avoid cheap insults. Separate the true part of the criticism from the propaganda overreach. Give the strongest public-facing questions, the best one-line counters, the honest caveats, the weak formulations to avoid, and the content pillars each question can become.

## Operating Prompt

> Turn the smear into a precise claim. Find the biggest counterexample. Make the counterexample impossible to ignore in one sentence. Avoid cleverness. Avoid overexplaining. Make the question true under pressure, simple enough to repeat, and strong enough to force the other side to clarify or retreat.

## Canonical Smear-Breakers

### 1. Smear: "Islam spread by the sword."

**Best current question:**

> If Islam spread mostly by force, why is the largest Muslim country on Earth a place no foreign Muslim conquest made Muslim?

**Alternate cold-friendly version:**

> Indonesia is the largest Muslim country on Earth. It was not made Muslim by invading Muslim armies. How does the slogan explain that?

**Best frame:**

> Conquest is not conversion.

**Honest caveat:**

Muslim empires conquered. Coercion happened in some places. Unequal incentives existed in some periods. But those facts do not prove that Islam's global spread is mostly explained by forced mass conversion.

**Unsafe wording to avoid:**

- "No Muslim army ever stepped foot in Indonesia." Too fragile.
- "Arab armies never conquered Indonesia." Too narrow if the opponent's claim is about Muslims generally.

**Safer wording:**

> No foreign Muslim conquest made Indonesia Muslim.

**Content pillar:**

> The largest Muslim country breaks the simplest version of the sword story.

---

### 2. Smear: "Islam is incompatible with modernity."

**S-tier question:**

> Which part is incompatible with the modern world — charity, prayer, fasting, sobriety, family responsibility, modesty, or not worshiping money?

**S-tier inversion:**

> Maybe Islam is not incompatible with addiction, loneliness, pornography, debt, family collapse, alcohol abuse, gambling, and worship of money. Maybe moral limits are medicine.

**S-tier line:**

> A society can be technologically advanced and morally exhausted at the same time.

**Best frame:**

> Islam is not anti-modern. It is anti-decay.

**Honest caveat:**

Do not treat "the West" as one monolith. The sharper target is secular consumer modernity, not Western people, science, medicine, engineering, or useful institutions.

**Content pillar:**

> Is Islam incompatible with modernity, or with modernity's harms?

---

### 3. Smear: "Islam is Arab imperialism."

**S-tier question:**

> If Islam is Arab imperialism, why are most Muslims not Arab?

**S-tier line:**

> Islam began in Arabia. It did not remain Arab property.

**Best frame:**

> Islam is universal, not ethnic.

**Honest caveat:**

Arab empires existed. Arabization happened in some regions. But Islam as a faith became deeply Persian, Turkish, African, South Asian, Malay, Balkan, and global.

**Content pillar:**

> Most Muslims are not Arab. That alone breaks the smear.

---

### 4. Smear: "Islam destroys culture."

**S-tier line:**

> One God. Not one culture.

**Best question:**

> If Islam destroys culture, why do Muslim societies look so different across Indonesia, Senegal, Bosnia, Turkey, Iran, Morocco, Malaysia, and India?

**Best frame:**

> Islam gave different peoples a shared center without making them identical.

**Honest caveat:**

Some rulers and movements suppressed local practices. But Islam's global history is not cultural sameness; it is unity with enormous diversity.

**Content pillar:**

> One faith, many civilizational expressions.

---

### 5. Smear: "Islam is anti-knowledge."

**Cold-friendly question:**

> If Islam is anti-knowledge, why did Muslim civilization help give the world algebra, optics, hospitals, libraries, observatories, and universities?

**Cold-friendly line:**

> A civilization that hates knowledge does not build hospitals, libraries, observatories, and universities.

**Best frame:**

> Knowledge was not only collected. It was institutionalized.

**Honest caveat:**

Muslim societies disagreed over philosophy, theology, censorship, and authority. But "Islam is anti-knowledge" cannot explain the civilizational fruit.

**Content pillar:**

> The civilization called backward helped build the modern knowledge inheritance.

---

### 6. Smear: "Islam cannot coexist."

**Best question:**

> If Muslim rule meant erasing everyone else, why did ancient Christian and Jewish communities survive for centuries under Muslim rule?

**Best line:**

> Survival is not the same as equality. But it destroys the myth of total erasure.

**Honest caveat:**

Premodern Muslim rule was not modern liberal equality. Non-Muslims often had protected but subordinate status. That is different from forced total erasure.

**Content pillar:**

> Not modern equality. Not total erasure. Tell the truth.

---

### 7. Smear: "Muslims hate Jesus / Christians."

**S-tier line:**

> Muslims do not worship Jesus. They still love and honor him.

**Best question:**

> If Islam hates Jesus, why does the Qur'an honor him as the Messiah?

**Best frame:**

> Islam disagrees with Christian theology. It does not erase Jesus.

**Honest caveat:**

Muslim-Christian history includes coexistence, debate, polemics, war, protection, oppression, alliance, and exchange. The history is not one emotion.

**Content pillar:**

> Islam is not anti-Jesus. It is radically pro-Jesus, but not Christian.

---

### 8. Smear: "The Prophet ﷺ was just a warlord."

**Best question:**

> If he was "just a warlord," why did the civilization he left behind organize daily life around prayer, charity, fasting, family, law, restraint, and accountability to God?

**Best line:**

> A warlord leaves behind a gang. A prophet leaves behind worship, law, charity, restraint, and civilization.

**Honest caveat:**

The Prophet ﷺ led a community that fought wars. That must be studied historically. Reducing him to "warlord" is not analysis; it is polemic.

**Content pillar:**

> War happened. Warlord is the wrong frame.

---

### 9. Smear: "Islam oppresses women."

**Best line:**

> The choice is not patriarchy or pornography. The question is dignity.

**Best question:**

> Are you criticizing Islam, patriarchy, bad law, bad culture, poverty, authoritarian states, or modernity's idea that liberation means becoming exploitable?

**Honest caveat:**

Muslim societies have real failures to answer for. Denying that is weak. But pretending secular consumer culture is pure liberation is also weak.

**Content pillar:**

> True dignity is not patriarchy or commodification.

---

### 10. Smear: "Islam is terrorism."

**S-tier line:**

> You do not get to define 2 billion people by the worst actors you selectively notice.

**Best question:**

> If terrorism defines Islam, do school shootings define Christianity, nuclear bombs define secular liberalism, and communist massacres define atheism?

**Best frame:**

> Condemn the crime. Reject the collective smear.

**Honest caveat:**

Muslim extremism exists and must be opposed. Collective blame is propaganda.

**Content pillar:**

> Condemn the crime. Refuse the collective smear.

---

### 11. Smear: "Sharia is barbaric."

**Best question:**

> Before calling Sharia barbaric, define what you mean: prayer, charity, family law, dietary rules, courts, punishments, or a dictator's abuse?

**Best line:**

> Most people attacking Sharia are attacking a word they cannot define.

**Honest caveat:**

There are serious debates about law, rights, governance, punishment, pluralism, and modern states. But "Sharia = barbarism" is not analysis.

**Content pillar:**

> Define before you demonize.

---

### 12. Smear: "Islam is backward."

**S-tier question:**

> Backward compared to what — addiction, loneliness, pornography, debt, broken families, alcohol abuse, gambling, and worship of money?

**S-tier line:**

> Sometimes "backward" just means refusing to decay in the approved direction.

**Best frame:**

> Moral restraint is not backward.

**Honest caveat:**

Muslim societies need reform, excellence, education, justice, and stronger institutions. But moral restraint is not decay.

**Content pillar:**

> Backward to decadence can be forward to sanity.

## Universal Response Sequence

Use this sequence in comments, scripts, essays, and debates:

1. **Name the smear.**
2. **Ask the load-bearing question.**
3. **Give one concrete counterexample.**
4. **Concede the true part.**
5. **Reject the overreach.**
6. **End with a portable line.**

Example:

> If Islam spread mostly by force, why is the largest Muslim country on Earth a place no foreign Muslim conquest made Muslim? Muslim empires did conquer. Coercion happened in some places. But conquest is not conversion, and Indonesia breaks the simplest version of the slogan.

## Core Maxim

> A slogan is not a source. A smear is not an argument.
