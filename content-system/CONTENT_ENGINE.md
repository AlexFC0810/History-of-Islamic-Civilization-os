# Content Engine

## Core Strategy

Do not lead with doctrinal argument.

Lead with curiosity, calibration, overlooked history, moral surprise, and cold-friendly load-bearing questions.

The operating standard:

> Source hardening before virality. Compressed truth with moral force.

## Primary Series

### What School Didn't Teach You

Format: historical recalibration.

Examples:

- Animal welfare before modernity.
- Medieval hygiene realities.
- Preservation of knowledge.
- Rules of war history.
- Hospitals, waqf, and public welfare.

### Load-Bearing Questions

Canonical file: `content-system/load-bearing-questions/README.md`

Format: smear -> stress test -> honest caveat -> portable line.

Purpose: break vague slogans without entering endless reactive debate.

Core line:

> A slogan is not a source. A smear is not an argument.

Examples:

- If Islam spread mostly by force, why is the largest Muslim country on Earth a place no foreign Muslim conquest made Muslim?
- Which part is incompatible with the modern world — charity, prayer, fasting, sobriety, family responsibility, modesty, or not worshiping money?
- If Islam is Arab imperialism, why are most Muslims not Arab?
- One God. Not one culture.
- You do not get to define 2 billion people by the worst actors you selectively notice.

---

### Islam as Protection

Canonical file: `content-system/islam-as-protection/README.md`

Format: shared pain -> zoom out -> social consequence -> Islamic limit as protection.

Purpose: reveal the wisdom behind Islamic limits without beginning with abstraction or defensiveness.

Core line:

> Islam looks restrictive when you only ask, "What do I want right now?" It starts looking wise when you ask, "What does this protect?"

Examples:

- One glass may look harmless. A drinking culture does not.
- Gambling turns hope into a business model.
- Modesty is not shame. It is refusing to make the body the price of attention.
- Fasting makes hunger a teacher.
- In Islam, charity is not branding. It is obligation.
- Prayer is not escape from reality. It is a daily refusal to worship the world.

## Content Unit Template

Each content unit should include:

1. **Hook:** one cold-friendly question or line.
2. **Concrete example:** one fact, story, institution, or lived pain.
3. **Reframe:** what the audience had been missing.
4. **Honest caveat:** the true part that should not be denied.
5. **Portable ending:** the line worth remembering.
6. **Source status:** ready / caveated / research only.

## North Star

> Build questions so strong that propaganda has to clarify, retreat, or reveal itself.
