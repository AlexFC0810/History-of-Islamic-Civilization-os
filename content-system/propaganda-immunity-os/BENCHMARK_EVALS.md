# Propaganda Immunity OS — Benchmark Evals

## Purpose

This file defines the benchmark/eval layer for Propaganda Immunity OS.

The system is only real if it improves outputs.

The practical test:

> Does this system produce better replies, better questions, better content, better understanding, and better results than a generic answer?

This file stores baseline replies, target standards, evaluation criteria, and simulation outputs so the system can recursively improve.

## Core Benchmark

A generic reply usually does one of four weak things:

1. gives a vague correction;
2. over-explains;
3. sounds defensive;
4. misses the propaganda mechanism.

A Propaganda Immunity OS reply should:

1. identify the damaged map;
2. correct the category error;
3. speak to the silent observer;
4. be cold-friendly;
5. stay true under pressure;
6. avoid dehumanizing back;
7. end with a line worth remembering.

## Scoring Rubric

Score each candidate 1–5.

1. **Cold-friendly:** understandable to a stranger in one pass.
2. **Map repair:** corrects the deeper false frame, not just the surface claim.
3. **Moral force:** carries dignity, seriousness, and weight.
4. **Compression:** short enough to travel.
5. **Truth under pressure:** avoids fragile or exaggerated claims.
6. **Observer effect:** gives silent readers a better way to see reality.
7. **Tone control:** hard on the lie, door open for the person.

Total possible score: 35.

### Tiers

- **S-tier:** 32–35. Canonical. Use broadly.
- **A-tier:** 28–31. Strong. Use with minor edits or context.
- **B-tier:** 23–27. Good idea, wording not final.
- **C-tier:** under 23. Keep as research scrap or discard.

## Simulation Protocol

For each claim or incident:

1. Write the generic baseline.
2. Diagnose why it is weak.
3. Run Propaganda Immunity OS.
4. Produce 3–5 candidate replies.
5. Score each candidate.
6. Pick the champion.
7. Name the failure modes.
8. Save the best line and what made it work.

## Benchmark Incident 001: Hijab Parking-Lot Harassment

### Incident

A Muslim woman in hijab is walking with her sister in a parking lot. A woman says, "nice costume." When challenged, she says, "go back to your country" or "go back where you came from."

### Propaganda Mechanism

- Hijab is reduced to costume.
- Muslim identity is coded as foreign.
- Belonging is denied.
- A person is treated as a symbol, not a neighbor.
- Private harassment is downstream of public propaganda.

### Generic Baseline Reply

> That is racist and Islamophobic. You should not say that.

### Why Baseline Is Weak

It is morally correct, but not maximally effective. It names the behavior but does not repair the map. It does not expose the assumption. It does not give observers a memorable frame.

## Candidate Replies

### Candidate A

> Hijab is not a costume. It is faith, modesty, and identity.

**Score:** 29/35  
**Tier:** A

**Strength:** Very cold-friendly and clean.  
**Weakness:** Does not directly address "go back to your country."

### Candidate B

> Muslim women are not foreigners by default. Many were born here. Many are citizens. All deserve basic dignity.

**Score:** 30/35  
**Tier:** A

**Strength:** Directly corrects foreignness assumption.  
**Weakness:** Slightly explanatory, less memorable.

### Candidate C

> Telling someone to "go back" because of a headscarf is not patriotism. It is ignorance wearing confidence.

**Score:** 33/35  
**Tier:** S

**Strength:** Cold-friendly, morally forceful, memorable, and exposes false patriotism.  
**Weakness:** Slightly sharp; best for public response rather than de-escalation.

### Candidate D

> You saw a woman in hijab and assumed foreigner. That is not insight. That is programming.

**Score:** 32/35  
**Tier:** S

**Strength:** Diagnoses the propaganda mechanism. Very memorable.  
**Weakness:** More confrontational; may close the door for the person but works well for observers.

### Candidate E

> The problem is not that she wore hijab. The problem is that you were taught to see a neighbor as an invader.

**Score:** 35/35  
**Tier:** S+

**Strength:** Repairs the map, humanizes the target, exposes propaganda, and speaks to observers.  
**Weakness:** None for public-facing commentary; may be too heavy for quick in-person de-escalation.

## Champion Reply

> The problem is not that she wore hijab. The problem is that you were taught to see a neighbor as an invader.

## Best Short Reply

> Telling someone to "go back" because of a headscarf is not patriotism. It is ignorance wearing confidence.

## Best Gentle Reply

> I think you may be mixing up faith, clothing, and nationality. Muslim women live here too. Many were born here. All deserve basic respect.

## Best Public Comment Reply

> She wore hijab and you saw a foreigner. That is not patriotism. That is a broken map. The problem is not her faith or clothing. The problem is that you were taught to see a neighbor as an invader.

## What Not To Say

Avoid:

> Americans are all ignorant.

Too broad and alienating.

Avoid:

> Go back to Europe.

This mirrors the dehumanizing frame instead of repairing it.

Avoid:

> You are evil.

May be emotionally satisfying, but it does not teach observers how propaganda worked.

## Benchmark Incident 002: "Islam Spread by the Sword"

### Generic Baseline Reply

> That is not true. Islam spread through peace and trade.

### Why Baseline Is Weak

It overcorrects. Muslim empires did conquer, and coercion happened in some contexts. A truth-first answer must concede conquest while separating conquest from conversion.

### Candidate A

> Conquest is not conversion.

**Score:** 29/35  
**Tier:** A

**Strength:** Very compressed.  
**Weakness:** Needs one example to be cold-friendly.

### Candidate B

> If Islam spread mostly by force, why is the largest Muslim country on Earth a place no foreign Muslim conquest made Muslim?

**Score:** 34/35  
**Tier:** S

**Strength:** Massive counterexample, cold-friendly, truthful under pressure if wording remains precise.  
**Weakness:** Needs source card for public education.

### Candidate C

> The slogan collapses conquest, rule, culture, law, incentives, and personal conversion into one word. That is not history. That is propaganda compression.

**Score:** 31/35  
**Tier:** A

**Strength:** Excellent map repair.  
**Weakness:** Less cold-friendly than Indonesia line.

## Champion Reply

> If Islam spread mostly by force, why is the largest Muslim country on Earth a place no foreign Muslim conquest made Muslim? Conquest happened. Coercion happened in some places. But conquest is not conversion, and Indonesia breaks the simplest version of the slogan.

## Benchmark Incident 003: "Islam Is Incompatible with Modernity"

### Generic Baseline Reply

> Islam is compatible with modernity because Muslims use technology and live in modern societies.

### Why Baseline Is Weak

It accepts the opponent's frame that modernity is the judge. It does not question what kind of modernity is being defended.

### Candidate A

> Which part is incompatible with the modern world — charity, prayer, fasting, sobriety, family responsibility, modesty, or not worshiping money?

**Score:** 35/35  
**Tier:** S+

### Candidate B

> Maybe Islam is not incompatible with addiction, loneliness, pornography, debt, family collapse, alcohol abuse, gambling, and worship of money. Maybe moral limits are medicine.

**Score:** 34/35  
**Tier:** S

### Candidate C

> A society can be technologically advanced and morally exhausted at the same time.

**Score:** 32/35  
**Tier:** S

## Champion Reply

> Which part is incompatible with the modern world — charity, prayer, fasting, sobriety, family responsibility, modesty, or not worshiping money? Maybe Islam is not the problem. Maybe moral limits look backward to a society that has normalized addiction, loneliness, debt, family collapse, alcohol abuse, gambling, and worship of money.

## Recursive Improvement Rule

Every benchmark should improve over time.

When a stronger line appears:

1. add it as a new candidate;
2. score it against the champion;
3. explain why it wins or loses;
4. preserve both if they serve different contexts;
5. update the champion only if the new line is stronger under the rubric.
