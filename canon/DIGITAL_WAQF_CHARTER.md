# Digital Waqf Charter
**Status:** PUBLIC CHARTER · public-benefit knowledge infrastructure
**Created:** 2026-06-27

## Purpose
This repository should function as a digital waqf: a durable public-benefit knowledge asset that preserves, tests, and shares source-led civilizational memory.

It should remain useful even when no single founder, editor, or contributor is actively pushing it forward.

## What this means
A digital waqf is not just content. It is a continuity system.

It should:

1. preserve beneficial knowledge,
2. make claims easier to inspect,
3. attach source trails and uncertainty levels,
4. remain exportable and readable outside one platform,
5. invite careful contribution without sacrificing rigor,
6. prioritize public benefit over personal attention,
7. protect the difference between honest confidence and overclaiming.

## Operating standard
This project exists to help people remember accurately, not to flatter identity or win arguments cheaply.

Strong claims should be:

- historically grounded,
- source-aware,
- fair to context,
- clear about uncertainty,
- useful for moral and civilizational calibration.

## Public promise
This repo should avoid two equal failures:

1. **Weakness:** hiding true, powerful historical claims because they sound inconvenient.
2. **Brittleness:** overstating claims until they cannot survive scrutiny.

The ideal is truthful strength.

## Persistence standard
GitHub is the working home, not the whole institution.

Important material should eventually have:

- Markdown source files,
- source trails,
- human-readable indexes,
- release/export bundles,
- backup and mirror notes,
- clear review status,
- maintainer succession guidance.

## Contribution posture
Contributors should improve clarity, sourcing, structure, and fairness.

A contribution is strongest when it helps a future reader answer:

- What is being claimed?
- How confident should we be?
- What is the evidence?
- What context changes the meaning?
- What is the strongest honest formulation?
- What should not be overstated?

## North star
Civilizational memory is not for pride alone. It is for pattern recovery.

The goal is not nostalgia. The goal is to recover durable lessons about knowledge, mercy, restraint, institution-building, accountability, and renewal.
