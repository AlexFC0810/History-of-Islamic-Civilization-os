# BEST SOURCES REGISTRY — History of Islamic Civilization (esp. the Golden Age)

Date: 2026-06-27
Lens: Metahistorian Standard ("Be more loyal to reality than to any narrative"). Loyal ONLY to truth — not to apology, not to polemic.

## How to use this registry

This is a sourcing hierarchy, not a reading list. The Source Hierarchy Discipline applies to **where a claim sits**: Revelation ≠ Prophetic model ≠ early community ≠ fiqh ≠ state law ≠ culture ≠ modern perception. A source is "best" only relative to the claim-level it can actually support.

Two rules govern everything below:

1. **Triangulate.** No single source — primary OR academic — is self-certifying. Anchor a claim in a primary source, confirm the reading with at least one reputable academic, and flag where they disagree.
2. **Name the leaning.** Every author has a school, a method, and blind spots. Naming them is not a slur; it is calibration. Western academic, Muslim-heritage, revisionist, and confessional sources each see things the others miss — and each has a characteristic over-reach.

## TIER 1 — PRIMARY SOURCES

These establish what was *said/revealed/legislated/recorded*, not what was *true on the ground*. A primary source proves doctrine, text, or a chronicler's claim — never automatically "lived practice." (Causality Discipline: a verse is the *standard*; whether society met it is a separate empirical question.)

### 1A. Revelation
- **The Qur'an** — cite as `surah:ayah` (e.g. `Qur'an 81:8–9`, `2:256`, `4:11`). Use a scholarly translation and name it: **M.A.S. Abdel Haleem** (Oxford, plain modern English, light footnoting), **The Study Quran** ed. Seyyed Hossein Nasr (HarperOne; rich classical-commentary apparatus, Perennialist editorial leaning — flag it), **Arberry** (literary, dated). *Watch for:* translation choices are interpretive; for any load-bearing word check 2+ translations and the Arabic. A verse proves the *source-level architecture* (Scale Discipline) — not that any society implemented it.

### 1B. Prophetic model — Hadith (with grading awareness)
- **The Kutub al-Sittah** (the six Sunni canonical collections, compiled c. 840–912 CE):
  - **Sahih al-Bukhari** (d. 870) and **Sahih Muslim** (d. 875) — the *Sahihayn*, the two that explicitly aimed to admit only authenticated reports; highest baseline reliability.
  - **Sunan Abu Dawud, Jami al-Tirmidhi, Sunan al-Nasa'i, Sunan Ibn Majah** — canonical but contain hadith of *mixed* grade; do not treat a Sunan report as automatically Sahih.
- **Citation convention:** collection + number, e.g. `Sahih al-Bukhari 2365`, `Sahih Muslim 1955a`, with the `sunnah.com/bukhari:2365` permalink. **Always state the grade** (sahih / hasan / da'if) — the grade lives in the *isnad* (chain), and the matn (text) is meaningless without it.
- **Shia hadith** (separate canon — use when the claim is Shia or comparative): the *Four Books* — **al-Kafi** (al-Kulayni), **Man la yahduruhu al-Faqih** (al-Saduq), **Tahdhib al-Ahkam** and **al-Istibsar** (al-Tusi). Do not silently merge Sunni and Shia canons.
- *Watch for:* (1) a single hadith ≠ "Islam says"; it is one node in fiqh. (2) Grading is itself contested across schools/scholars. (3) Cherry-picking either way — the animal-mercy hadith and the harsh hadd hadith are *both* in the canon; honesty requires holding both.

### 1C. Classical chronicles & adab (historiography by Muslims, for the record)
- **al-Tabari (d. 923), *Tarikh al-Rusul wa'l-Muluk*** — the master annalistic chronicle; SUNY translated it into ~40 English volumes (the single most useful primary entry-point). *Watch for:* al-Tabari preserves *multiple, contradictory* reports without adjudicating — he is an archive, not a verdict. Treat each report as a *claim*, graded by isnad.
- **Ibn Khaldun (d. 1406), *The Muqaddimah*** (trans. Franz Rosenthal) — the foundational work of social history / proto-sociology (asabiyya, the cyclical rise-and-fall of dynasties). Brilliant analytic frame; *watch for:* his cyclical model is a *theory*, not data — don't import it as fact.
- Others by domain: **al-Mas'udi** (*Muruj al-Dhahab*), **al-Baladhuri** (*Futuh al-Buldan* — the conquests), **Ibn Ishaq/Ibn Hisham** (the *Sira* — earliest biography, but at 2+ centuries' remove and heavily redacted; the revisionists' chief target), **al-Waqidi** (*Maghazi*), **Ibn Sa'd** (*Tabaqat*), **Miskawayh**, **al-Ya'qubi**.
- *Universal caveat for this sub-tier:* these are **near-but-not-contemporary** to the events they narrate (most written 150–300 years after early Islam). Revisionist scholarship (see 2C) exists precisely because of this gap. Use chronicles to establish *what the tradition recorded*, then cross-check against documentary, numismatic, archaeological, and non-Muslim contemporary evidence.

### 1D. Classical legal & scientific texts (the "fruit," in the authors' own words)
- **Law/fiqh:** al-Shafi'i's *Risala* (legal theory's foundation), al-Marghinani's *Hidaya* (Hanafi), al-Mawardi's *al-Ahkam al-Sultaniyya* (governance), Ibn Rushd's *Bidayat al-Mujtahid* (comparative fiqh). Distinguish *fiqh* (jurists' reasoning) from *state law* (what a ruler enforced) — they are different hierarchy levels.
- **Science/philosophy (Golden Age core):** Ibn Sina/Avicenna (*Canon of Medicine*, *Kitab al-Shifa*), al-Khwarizmi (algebra/algorithms), al-Razi/Rhazes (medicine), Ibn al-Haytham/Alhazen (*Book of Optics*), al-Biruni, Ibn Rushd/Averroes, al-Farabi, al-Ghazali (*Ihya*, *Tahafut*), Ibn al-Nafis (pulmonary circulation), Jabir ibn Hayyan. *Watch for:* the "X invented Y" trap (Public Language Standard) — most were *synthesizers and advancers* of Greek, Persian, and Indian inheritance, which is itself a major achievement. State contributions **narrow and defensible**, not as origination myths.

## TIER 2 — MODERN ACADEMIC HISTORIANS (named, across the spectrum)

The Symmetry rule applies to the *bibliography itself*: read across schools so no single method's blind spot becomes yours. Leanings noted are calibration, not dismissal.

### 2A. Western academic — mainstream "traditionalist-critical"
*(accept the Islamic sources as broadly usable but read them critically)*
- **Hugh Kennedy** (SOAS) — the standard narrative historian of the early caliphate and conquests (*The Great Arab Conquests*, *The Prophet and the Age of the Caliphates*, *The Court of the Caliphs*). Lucid, evidence-led, accessible. *Note:* moved from anti-revisionist toward "the old certainties have disappeared" between editions — a model of updating to evidence. Best single entry-point for the Golden Age political frame.
- **Marshall Hodgson** (d. 1968), *The Venture of Islam* (3 vols) — the towering interpretive synthesis; coined "Islamicate" (the *civilization* shaped by Islam, incl. non-Muslims) and "Persianate." *Watch for:* dense, idiosyncratic vocabulary; mid-20th-c.; sympathetic-civilizational framing — strong on coherence, lighter on the failures.
- **Ira M. Lapidus**, *A History of Islamic Societies* — the standard one-volume social-history survey. (This is "Lapidus," not "S.A.M.") Balanced, institution-focused.
- **Chase F. Robinson** — *Islamic Historiography* (essential on *how* to read the chronicles and their reliability) and *Islamic Civilization in Thirty Lives*. Methodologically careful, source-critical without being nihilist.
- **Jonathan Berkey**, *The Formation of Islam* — excellent on the slow, contested "formation" rather than instant fully-formed religion.
- **Fred M. Donner** (*Muhammad and the Believers*, *Narratives of Islamic Origins*) — bridges traditionalist and revisionist; argues early Islam was an ecumenical "Believers' movement." Influential, contested.

### 2B. Western academic — Islamic LAW and the SCIENCES specialists
- **Jonathan A.C. Brown** (Georgetown) — *Hadith: Muhammad's Legacy*, *Misquoting Muhammad*, *Slavery and Islam*. The most accessible rigorous Western authority on hadith and on hard topics (slavery, sex, consent) — does *not* flinch from the ugly questions, which is the value. *Watch for:* a **convert/Muslim** scholar who is sometimes read as apologetic on the hardest cases (his slavery book drew real academic criticism); use him for the source-critical machinery, cross-check his conclusions on contested ethics against secular critics. Flag the leaning, keep the rigor.
- **Wael Hallaq** (Columbia) — *The Origins and Evolution of Islamic Law*, *Sharia: Theory, Practice, Transformations*, *The Impossible State*. The leading theorist of Islamic law's structure and its rupture under the modern state. *Watch for:* a normative, critical-of-modernity thesis (the modern nation-state is incompatible with Sharia's moral logic) — a powerful *argument*, read it as such.
- **George Saliba** (Columbia) — *Islamic Science and the Making of the European Renaissance*. The leading historian of Arabic/Islamic astronomy; demolishes the "Golden Age was just translation/preservation, then decline" cliché and documents original theoretical work (the *Maragha* school, Tusi-couple → Copernicus question). *Watch for:* corrective enthusiasm — he is *arguing against* a decline narrative, so weigh his strongest "influence on Europe" claims (e.g. Copernicus) as still-debated.
- **Khaled Abou El Fadl** (UCLA) — Islamic law, authority, tolerance, and its abuse (*Rebellion and Violence in Islamic Law*, *The Great Theft*). A jurist-scholar; reformist Muslim voice strong on how authoritarianism hijacks legal language (directly serves the Causality Discipline: Islam as *cover* vs *cause*).
- **A.I. Sabra**, **David A. King**, **Roshdi Rashed** — historians of Islamic science/mathematics/astronomy; technical, authoritative. Rashed especially on mathematics.

### 2C. Western academic — the REVISIONIST school
*(skeptical of the Islamic literary sources; reconstruct from coins, inscriptions, archaeology, and non-Muslim contemporary texts)*
- **Patricia Crone** (d. 2015) — *Hagarism* (w/ Michael Cook; her own later partial walk-back), *Meccan Trade*, *Slaves on Horses*, *God's Rule*. "Most celebrated and vilified" scholar of Islamic origins. The indispensable adversarial check on naïve use of the chronicles. *Watch for:* early hyper-skepticism that even she softened; treat *Hagarism* as a provocation, her mature work as serious.
- **Michael Cook**, **John Wansbrough** (d. 2002; Qur'anic-origins skepticism — very technical, much contested), **Fred Donner** (the moderate wing). 
- *Why include them:* they keep the registry honest about the **150–300-year source gap**. *Watch for:* the school can over-correct into "we can know almost nothing," which the documentary and epigraphic record increasingly *does not* support. Use revisionists as a stress-test, not a replacement.

### 2D. Muslim-world / heritage / non-Western scholarship
*(centered in the tradition; ranges from rigorous to advocacy — grade individually, do NOT treat "Muslim author" as a uniform tier)*
- **Salma Khadra Jayyusi** (ed.) — *The Legacy of Muslim Spain*, *The City in the Islamic World*; founder of PROTA. Monumental multi-author heritage scholarship on al-Andalus and Islamic urbanism.
- **Aziz Al-Azmeh** — *Islams and Modernities*, *The Emergence of Islam in Late Antiquity*. A secular, theoretically sharp Arab historian; explicitly *anti-essentialist* (note his title: "Islams," plural). Strong corrective to "Islam-as-monolith" from *within* the heritage.
- **Muhammad Hamidullah** — meticulous documentary work on the earliest Islamic state/treaties (heritage-confessional but source-anchored).
- **Marshall-adjacent / Arab academy:** **Mohammed Arkoun** (critical, "applied Islamology"), **Fazlur Rahman** (modernist reformer, *Islam*), **Seyyed Hossein Nasr** (traditionalist/Perennialist — superb on science-and-spirituality, *flag the Perennialist lens*).
- **Sub-continental & global:** scholars publishing through Brill, Cambridge, OUP from Turkish, Iranian, Egyptian, Pakistani, Malaysian institutions. *General watch:* (a) distinguish *academic-historical* from *da'wa/apologetic* output even by credentialed authors; (b) state-funded heritage scholarship (e.g. some Gulf/Turkish institutional output) can carry a triumphalist tilt — grade the work, not the passport.

### Spectrum at a glance (read across, not down)
| Lens | Use it for | Characteristic over-reach |
|---|---|---|
| Mainstream Western (Kennedy, Hodgson, Lapidus, Robinson) | The reliable baseline narrative | mid-century civilizational sympathy / Eurocentric framing |
| Law & science specialists (Brown, Hallaq, Saliba, Abou El Fadl) | Deep, topic-specific rigor | corrective enthusiasm; some apologetic pull (Brown on slavery) |
| Revisionists (Crone, Cook, Wansbrough) | Stress-testing the source gap | "we can know nothing" over-skepticism |
| Muslim-heritage (Jayyusi, Al-Azmeh, Hamidullah, Nasr, Rahman) | Inside-the-tradition depth & anti-essentialism | triumphalism / confessional or Perennialist framing |

## TIER 3 — REPUTABLE INSTITUTIONS, DATABASES & ENCYCLOPEDIAS

- **Encyclopaedia of Islam (Brill)** — the field's standard reference. **EI2** (1954–2005, complete) and **EI3** (2007–, ongoing, peer-reviewed by ≥2 expert readers, broader/modern coverage). The default first-stop for any term, person, dynasty, or concept. *Watch for:* EI2 articles can be dated; prefer the EI3 entry when it exists. Paywalled (Brill Reference Works Online).
- **Brill** generally (incl. *The Biographical Encyclopaedia of Astronomers*, journals like *Islamic Law and Society*, *Arabica*) — premier academic publisher in the field.
- **Cambridge & Oxford University Presses** — *The New Cambridge History of Islam* (6 vols) and *The Oxford History of Islam* (ed. Esposito) as authoritative multi-author surveys; *watch:* Esposito's editorial voice is sometimes critiqued as sympathetic — fine, just calibrated.
- **sunnah.com** — the practical hadith database; clean permalinks, Arabic + translation + grades for the major collections. *Watch for:* it is an **aggregator**, not a critical edition — Arabic sourced from secondary digital texts, translations of varying provenance, "About" page thin on attribution. Use it to *locate and cite*, then verify wording/grade against a print critical edition or EI/Brown for anything load-bearing.
- **al-islam.org (Ahlul Bayt DILP)** — large, well-organized **Shia (Twelver)** digital library. Excellent *for Shia sources and perspective* — and label it as such; do not cite it as a neutral "Islam" source for Sunni-default claims.
- **Quran resources:** quran.com / corpus.quran.com (Quranic Arabic Corpus — morphology, multiple translations side-by-side) for citation and cross-translation checks.
- **Muslim Heritage / FSTC (1001 Inventions)** — useful *leads* and accessible summaries on science history. *Watch for:* **advocacy-flavored** ("1001 Inventions" leans celebratory and has been criticized for over-claiming firsts) — treat as a lead-generator to verify against Saliba/Rashed/EI, never as proof.
- **Academic indexes:** *Index Islamicus* (the bibliography of the field), JSTOR, the *Journal of the American Oriental Society*, *Der Islam*, *Bulletin of SOAS*.

## TIER 4 — SOURCES TO TREAT WITH CAUTION (named genres, with the *why*)

The Standard requires symmetry here: **both** poles distort. Do not use either as *proof*; at most, as a lead to verify against Tiers 1–3.

### 4A. Apologetic / advocacy (the over-claim risk → triumphalism + cleaned-up history)
- **Da'wa and apologetics sites/channels** — e.g. apologetics blogs, "miracles of the Quran," debate-circuit content, and the celebratory edge of heritage-promotion outfits. *Why caution:* they select the flattering hadith/fact, skip the failures, and slide into "Islam invented X / was uniquely humane / ended slavery in a generation" — exactly the overclaims the Public Language Standard bans. *Use:* as a *lead* ("is there really a hadith about X?") → then verify the text, grade, and context in Tier 1/2. Never cite as authority.
- *Also here:* state/institution-funded heritage media with a national-pride mandate. Real scholarship can hide inside it — extract the citation, drop the framing.

### 4B. Polemical / Islamophobic (the smear risk → reducing Islam to its worst)
- **WikiIslam** — academic-styled but, per peer-reviewed analysis (Oslo Met, 2024), an exercise in the "scientification of Islamophobia": biased topic-selection, near-zero neutral/positive coverage, weaponized as a citation by bigoted sites. *Why caution:* it often quotes *real* primary text but strips context, grade, and counter-evidence — the textbook Causality/Scale error (edge case → essence). *Use:* only to find *which* primary text a critic is leaning on, then read that text properly in Tier 1.
- **Answering-Islam** — Christian-missionary polemic; thoroughly *referenced* (its self-description), which is exactly the trap: footnoted ≠ fair. Confessional counter-apologetics, not history.
- **Bat Ye'or ("dhimmitude")** and the **Robert Spencer / "counter-jihad"** genre — popular polemics, not accepted academic history; "dhimmitude" as a totalizing concept is rejected by mainstream historians (who document dhimmi status honestly as *real legal subordination, not modern equality, but also not uniform persecution*). *Why caution:* they convert a true narrow claim (dhimmis had legal disabilities) into a civilizational verdict.
- *General watch:* any source whose output is *monodirectionally* negative (or positive) across every topic is doing prosecution/defense, not history.

### 4C. Untriaged AI / open-wiki / aggregator content
- **Wikipedia** — good *orientation and bibliography*, variable per-article; follow its footnotes to Tier 2/3, cite those. **Grokipedia / AI-generated encyclopedias** and unsourced "fact" channels — orientation only, verify everything. Never a primary citation.

## The integrity discipline applied to sourcing (do this for every card)

1. **Locate the level** — is the claim about Revelation, Prophetic model, fiqh, state law, culture, or modern perception? Pick the source-tier that can actually support *that* level.
2. **Anchor primary, confirm academic** — Qur'an/hadith/chronicle + a named Tier-2 historian; note their leaning.
3. **Cross-school check** — if the claim is contested, cite a mainstream *and* a revisionist *and* (where relevant) a heritage reading; show where they diverge.
4. **Grade it A/B/C/D** — A = primary + academic consensus; B = primary solid, broader civilizational inference; C = contested / thin; D = advocacy-only or polemic-only. Hedge C/D explicitly.
5. **Refuse both over-reaches** — no "uniquely humane / invented / ended slavery in a generation" (triumphalism), no "Islam is X" from an edge case (smear).

> **Canon for this registry:** Truth first. Sources ranked. Read across the spectrum. Name every leaning. Verify before you cite. The goal is not Muslim pride or Western critique — it is to make reality harder to manipulate.
