# Source Card 003: Animal Mercy

Date: 2026-06-25

## Status

**Deployment status:** v1 source-hardened for low-risk public use with caveats.

**Best use:** respond to smears such as “Islam is barbaric,” “Islam is harsh,” or “Islam has no mercy.”

**House style:** concrete Prophetic examples first; no exclusivity claim; no sentimental overstatement.

## Claim

The Prophetic tradition contains concrete teachings of mercy toward animals: a woman is punished for imprisoning/starving a cat, a person is forgiven for giving water to a thirsty dog, reward is promised for serving every living being, living creatures should not be used as targets, and even slaughter is governed by ihsan.

This does not prove Muslims always treated animals well. It proves that the “Islam is only harshness” smear cannot explain the source-level animal mercy architecture.

## Source Anchors

### Sahih al-Bukhari 2365 — woman punished for starving/imprisoning a cat

Sunnah.com summary:

> A woman was punished because she kept a cat locked up until it died of hunger; she neither fed/watered it nor released it to eat.

Source: https://sunnah.com/bukhari:2365

### Sahih al-Bukhari 3321 — woman forgiven for giving water to a thirsty dog

Sunnah.com summary:

> A woman was forgiven after giving water to a panting dog that was near death from thirst.

Source: https://sunnah.com/bukhari:3321

### Sahih al-Bukhari 6009 — reward for serving every living being

Sunnah.com summary:

> A man gave water to a thirsty dog and was forgiven. When asked whether there is reward in serving animals, the Prophet ﷺ said there is reward in serving every living being.

Source: https://sunnah.com/bukhari:6009

### Sahih Muslim 1955a — ihsan in slaughter

Sunnah.com summary:

> Allah has enjoined goodness/ihsan in everything; when slaughtering, slaughter well, sharpen the blade, and let the animal die comfortably.

Source: https://sunnah.com/muslim:1955a

## Evidence Grade

**A** for the hadith anchors listed above as public-facing Sahih references.

**B** for the broader civilizational claim that Islamic law and practice generated durable animal-welfare norms; this needs separate fiqh/history source cards.

## Honest Caveats

1. Do not claim Muslims have always treated animals well.
2. Do not claim Islam is the only tradition with animal mercy.
3. Do not use animal mercy to dodge hard questions about slaughter, meat, hunting, or legal debates.
4. Do not imply the hadith abolish animal use; they regulate power through mercy and accountability.
5. Do not overstate “animal rights” in a modern legal sense unless defining terms carefully.

## Unsafe Wording to Avoid

- “Only Islam cares about animals.”
- “Muslims never mistreat animals.”
- “Islam is vegan/vegetarian by essence.”
- “Islam invented animal rights.”
- “Islamic slaughter is always painless.”

## Safe Wording

> The Prophetic tradition contains concrete teachings of animal mercy, including punishment for starving a cat, forgiveness for giving water to a thirsty dog, reward for serving living beings, and ihsan even in slaughter.

## Counterattack Simulation

### Counter: “But Muslims slaughter animals.”

Response:

> Yes. Islam permits animal slaughter, so do not misrepresent the tradition as modern vegetarianism. The point is that even permitted animal use is placed under ihsan, restraint, and accountability.

### Counter: “Muslim countries still abuse animals.”

Response:

> Many Muslims and Muslim societies fall short. That is a reason to revive the Prophetic standard, not a reason to erase it.

### Counter: “Other religions also teach animal mercy.”

Response:

> Correct. The claim is not exclusivity. The claim is that Islam's own sources contain powerful animal mercy teachings that the ‘barbaric Islam’ caricature cannot explain.

### Counter: “This is cherry-picking nice hadiths.”

Response:

> It is a direct counterexample to an overbroad smear. If the claim is “Islam is only harshness,” then source-level examples of mercy toward cats, dogs, and living beings matter.
