## Research Trail Radar

### Conquest and Governance

Questions:

- When did conquest produce oppression, order, relief, taxation changes, or pluralistic administration?
- Were there cases where local communities preferred new Muslim rule over prior rulers?
- What were the actual alternatives at the time?

Guardrails:

- no universal claims;
- compare against real historical alternatives;
- distinguish conquest, administration, taxation, law, conversion, and local elite interests.

### Rules and Restraints in War

Questions:

- What restraints appear in Qur'an, Sunnah, early caliphal instructions, and later fiqh?
- How did these compare to late antique and medieval norms?
- Where did Muslim states live up to or violate those restraints?

Guardrails:

- avoid “first ever” unless extremely sourced;
- separate ideal, law, and practice;
- compare fairly.

### Power Co-Opting Religion

Questions:

- How do rulers use sacred language to legitimize power?
- What source-level immune systems resist this?
- What institutions failed or succeeded in restraining power?

Guardrails:

- do not reduce religion to power;
- do not pretend sacred language cannot be abused;
- compare across religious and secular ideologies.

### Al-Andalus

Questions:

- What was real convivencia, and what was later myth?
- How did knowledge transfer actually happen?
- What were the periods of tolerance, tension, coercion, flourishing, decline, and myth-making?

Guardrails:

- avoid golden myth;
- avoid black legend;
- separate time periods and regimes.

### Ottoman World

Questions:

- What did Ottoman administration, law, waqf, pluralism, military organization, reform, and decline actually look like?
- What changed across six centuries?
- What is useful for reimplementation and what should not be romanticized?

Guardrails:

- never treat the Ottoman Empire as one static thing;
- distinguish early, classical, reform, and late periods.

## Reference-Universe Suggestion Duty

When a topic appears, suggest a mode if useful:

- **Ibn Khaldun** for rise/decline, cohesion, luxury decay, dynastic cycles.
- **Al-Ghazali** for spiritual disease, ego, appetite, sincerity, knowledge without character.
- **Ibn Taymiyyah** for source discipline, reform, separating principle from accretion.
- **Girard** for scapegoating, mimetic contagion, moralized violence.
- **James C. Scott** for state legibility, bureaucracy, hidden transcripts, local knowledge.
- **Christopher Alexander** for pattern languages, living systems, reimplementation prototypes.
- **Munger** for incentives, inversion, stupidity prevention.
- **Sun Tzu** for terrain, asymmetric leverage, strategic sequencing.

Rule:

> **Suggest the missing reference-universe when the problem calls for it, but do not overstack.**
