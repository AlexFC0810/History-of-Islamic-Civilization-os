# Question Engine

## Purpose

This file defines the questions the steward should ask by default.

The user often says, “I don’t know what I don’t know.” The agent’s job is to ask the questions that unlock the next map.

## Primary Question

> **What is the most truthful, useful, source-aware map of this topic?**

## The 12 Core Questions

### 1. What is the false map?

What story do people inherit that is too simple, false, distorted, incomplete, or strategically useful to someone?

### 2. What is the better map?

What frame explains more of the evidence with fewer distortions?

### 3. What are the load-bearing facts?

Which facts actually change the interpretation?

### 4. What are the hidden variables?

What is missing from the common narrative: finance, geography, institutions, technology, law, incentives, trauma, media, translation, demographics, ecology, or spiritual worldview?

### 5. What are the causal loops?

What reinforced what? What degraded what? What fed back into the system?

### 6. What institutions made this possible?

Do not stop at great people. Ask what infrastructure, laws, funding, schools, networks, cities, media, markets, or rituals made the outcome durable.

### 7. What role did memory play?

How was the event remembered, forgotten, mythologized, flattened, weaponized, or erased?

### 8. What is the strongest counterargument?

What would a serious critic say? What would change the thesis?

### 9. What is fact, interpretation, hypothesis, and poetic line?

Never let rhetoric masquerade as evidence.

### 10. What should be source-hardened before publication?

Identify the claims most likely to be challenged.

### 11. What is the reimplementation lesson?

What does this teach a builder, educator, donor, institution, founder, scholar, policymaker, or community?

### 12. What artifact should this become?

Canon file, memory file, source ledger, visual, debate response, essay, script, issue, or blueprint?

## Question Modes

### When exploring history

Ask:

- What happened?
- How do we know?
- What caused it?
- What else was happening?
- What changed afterward?
- How was it remembered?

### When exploring civilization

Ask:

- What operating loops were present?
- What institutions carried the values?
- What funded the system?
- What constrained power?
- What protected the vulnerable?
- What transmitted knowledge?

### When exploring decline

Ask:

- Which loops broke?
- Which loops were attacked?
- Which loops failed to adapt?
- Which failures were internal?
- Which pressures were external?
- What could be rebuilt first?

### When exploring renewal

Ask:

- What would matter in 100 years?
- What institution is missing?
- What capital is misrouted?
- What knowledge is trapped?
- What media map is false?
- What would a modern waqf fund?

## Final Rule

> **Better questions produce better maps. Better maps produce better civilization-building.**
