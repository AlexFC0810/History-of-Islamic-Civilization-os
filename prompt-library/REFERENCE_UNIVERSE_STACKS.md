# Reference Universe Stacks

Date: 2026-06-26

## Purpose

This file turns `canon/REFERENCE_UNIVERSES_AND_CREATIVE_APERTURE.md` into reusable prompt stacks.

Use these when the goal is discovery, synthesis, naming, reframing, or breakthrough-level thinking.

## Prime Rule

> **Use references as operating modes, not decorative name-drops.**

A strong stack should usually include:

1. **Method** — how to think.
2. **Power/Ambition** — how deeply to go.
3. **Domain/Truth Frame** — where to aim.

Formula:

```text
[Method] + [Power/Ambition] + [Domain/Truth Frame]
```

## Core Stack: Sherlock + ASI + Metahistorian

Best for:

- civilizational synthesis;
- hidden architecture;
- category-collapse detection;
- load-bearing questions;
- canon breakthroughs.

Prompt:

```text
Use Sherlock-level inference, ASI-level synthesis, and metahistorian truth discipline. Look for the hidden architecture underneath this thread. Do not merely summarize. Be bold but caveat-disciplined. Preserve any breakthrough as canon.
```

Why it works:

- Sherlock = hidden mechanism;
- ASI = synthesis ceiling;
- Metahistorian = long-arc truth frame.

## Ibn Khaldun Stack

Best for:

- rise and decline;
- social cohesion;
- asabiyyah;
- state formation;
- luxury decay;
- civilizational cycles.

Prompt:

```text
Use Ibn Khaldun-level civilizational diagnosis, systems thinking, and metahistorian truth discipline. Identify the cohesion loops, luxury-decay patterns, institutional incentives, and decline mechanisms underneath this topic. What is the rise/decline pattern trying to reveal?
```

Use with care:

- do not reduce everything to asabiyyah;
- test against evidence;
- distinguish analogy from historical proof.

## Al-Ghazali Stack

Best for:

- spiritual diagnosis;
- moral psychology;
- inner corruption;
- knowledge vs ego;
- appetite, sincerity, and purification;
- limits of rationalism.

Prompt:

```text
Use Al-Ghazali-level spiritual diagnosis, moral psychology, and metahistorian discipline. Look beneath the behavior into the diseases of the heart, incentives, self-deceptions, and knowledge-without-sincerity patterns. What is the inner mechanism?
```

Use with care:

- do not psychologize away material causes;
- keep source discipline;
- distinguish spiritual metaphor from empirical claim.

## Ibn Taymiyyah Stack

Best for:

- source discipline;
- anti-corruption analysis;
- distinguishing revelation from later accretion;
- legal/theological rigor;
- reform vs distortion.

Prompt:

```text
Use Ibn Taymiyyah-style source discipline, anti-corruption rigor, and metahistorian caution. Separate source from accretion, principle from abuse, and law from power. What must be purified, what must be preserved, and what claims need harder evidence?
```

Use with care:

- avoid polemical excess;
- do not flatten complex traditions;
- preserve adab and caveat discipline.

## René Girard Stack

Best for:

- mimetic desire;
- scapegoating;
- sacred violence;
- social contagion;
- rivalry;
- propaganda mobs.

Prompt:

```text
Use Girard-level mimetic analysis, Sherlock-level mechanism detection, and truth-first moral discipline. Where are people imitating desire, creating scapegoats, sacralizing violence, or hiding rivalry under moral language?
```

Use with care:

- do not force every conflict into scapegoating;
- identify actual incentives and institutions too.

## James C. Scott Stack

Best for:

- state legibility;
- bureaucracy;
- hidden transcripts;
- local knowledge;
- why top-down systems fail;
- state vs community tensions.

Prompt:

```text
Use James C. Scott-level state-legibility analysis, systems thinking, and metahistorian truth discipline. What is being simplified for control? What local knowledge is being erased? What do official narratives miss? Where is the hidden transcript?
```

Use with care:

- do not romanticize local practice;
- state capacity can also protect people;
- compare costs and benefits.

## Christopher Alexander Stack

Best for:

- living systems;
- pattern languages;
- institution design;
- architecture of flourishing;
- reimplementation prototypes.

Prompt:

```text
Use Christopher Alexander-style pattern language, systems design, and civilizational reimplementation thinking. What living patterns produced the fruit? What dead patterns replaced them? What modern prototype could restore aliveness at small scale?
```

Use with care:

- turn beauty into operational patterns;
- avoid vague organic language without implementation.

## Charlie Munger Stack

Best for:

- incentives;
- inversion;
- mental models;
- decision quality;
- avoiding stupidity;
- founder strategy.

Prompt:

```text
Use Charlie Munger-level inversion, incentives analysis, and multidisciplinary mental models. Where are we being stupid? What incentives dominate behavior? What would we avoid if we wanted this system to fail? What is the simplest high-leverage correction?
```

Use with care:

- not everything is incentives;
- moral/spiritual causes may also matter.

## Sun Tzu Stack

Prompt:

```text
Use Sun Tzu-level terrain reading, asymmetric strategy, and metahistorian restraint. What is the terrain? Where is leverage? What battles should be avoided? What position makes victory easier before action begins?
```

Use with care:

- neglected truths.

Prompt:

```text
Use Peter Thiel-style contrarian truth discovery, zero-to-one framing, and truth-first discipline. What important truth is almost nobody acting on? What hidden monopoly of insight exists here? What would be obvious in hindsight but invisible now?
```

## Prompt Selection Rule

Use:

- **Original Sherlock + ASI + Metahistorian** for wonder and hidden architecture.
- **Hybrid Universe + Guardrail** for breakthrough plus canon readiness.
- **Munger / Sun Tzu** for execution and strategy.
- **Ibn Khaldun / Christopher Alexander** for civilizational loops and reimplementation.
- **Girard / Scott** for propaganda, scapegoating, state narratives, and hidden transcripts.
- **Al-Ghazali / Ibn Taymiyyah** for spiritual diagnosis and source discipline.

## Final Canon

> **The right reference-universe is not the smartest-sounding name. It is the mode that best fits the hidden problem.**
