# Content Engine

## Purpose

Turn the Prophetic Age Seed OS into repeatable public content.

## Content Pillars

### 1. Seed before fruit

**Core line:** Study the seed to understand the fruit.

Hooks:

- The Golden Ages did not appear from nowhere.
- Before there were libraries, there was revelation.
- Before there were institutions, there was formation.

### 2. Mecca to Medina

**Core line:** Mecca formed the inner world. Medina formed the public world.

Hooks:

- The first operating system was built in two phases.
- Mecca built souls. Medina built social architecture.

### 3. Moral economy

**Core line:** Wealth was not rejected. It was placed under responsibility.

Hooks:

- Islam did not attack wealth. It attacked the worship of wealth.
- A market without trust becomes organized suspicion.

### 4. Knowledge seed

**Core line:** The libraries began with knowledge becoming sacred.

Hooks:

- The Golden Ages began before the libraries.
- A civilization of books begins with a command to learn, remember, and transmit.

### 5. Mercy as architecture

**Core line:** Mercy became social architecture.

Hooks:

- Mercy becomes powerful when it becomes duty, care, and public obligation.
- A civilization is judged by what it protects.

## Short-Form Script Template

1. Hook: make a map-changing claim.
2. False map: show what people usually miss.
3. Seed correction: explain the early seed.
4. Fruit connection: show what later scaled.
5. Close: return to Civilization OS.

## Carousel Template

Slide 1: Hook claim.  
Slide 2: False map.  
Slide 3: Seed principle.  
Slide 4: Later fruit.  
Slide 5: Common objection.  
Slide 6: Safer wording.  
Slide 7: Why it matters now.  
Slide 8: Reimplementation lesson.

## Guardrails

- No overclaiming.
- No flattening complexity.
- No using weak reports as hard proof.
- No treating every later society as perfect fruit.
- No image depiction of the Prophet.
- Source-harden before virality.

## North Star

> **The seed explains the fruit.**
