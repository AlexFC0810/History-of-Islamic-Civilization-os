# Claims and Proof Cards

Use this file to turn the Prophetic Age Seed OS into defensible public content.

## Claim Card Format

1. Claim
2. Evidence grade
3. Proof spine
4. Caveat
5. Safe public wording
6. Unsafe public wording
7. Content angle

---

## 1. Seed to fruit

**Claim:** The Golden Ages scaled what the Prophetic age seeded.

**Evidence grade:** B as synthesis.

**Proof spine:** Early principles around knowledge, charity, markets, law, worship, community, and mercy later scale into institutions.

**Caveat:** Later institutions also depended on empire, patronage, translation, urban wealth, and historical contingencies.

**Safe wording:** Many later Islamic institutions can be studied as scaled expressions of earlier seed principles.

**Unsafe wording:** The Golden Ages were mechanically caused by the Prophetic age alone.

**Content angle:** Study the seed to understand the fruit.

---

## 2. Mecca and Medina

**Claim:** Mecca formed the inner world; Medina formed the public world.

**Evidence grade:** B as interpretive lens.

**Proof spine:** Meccan emphasis on monotheism, patience, accountability, and formation; Medinan emphasis on community, law, charity, markets, family, and public order.

**Caveat:** Both periods contain spiritual and social dimensions.

**Safe wording:** As a teaching lens, Mecca emphasizes inner formation while Medina emphasizes public formation.

**Unsafe wording:** Mecca was only spiritual and Medina was only political.

**Content angle:** Before a civilization had institutions, it had formed souls.

---

## 3. Moral economy

**Claim:** The Prophetic age planted a moral economy.

**Evidence grade:** B.

**Proof spine:** Wealth as trust, giving, market ethics, contracts, inheritance, and concern for the vulnerable.

**Caveat:** Economic systems changed across later regions and centuries.

**Safe wording:** The Prophetic age framed wealth inside responsibility, community obligation, and accountability.

**Unsafe wording:** Every later Islamic economy perfectly embodied the Prophetic model.

**Content angle:** The economic seed was not anti-wealth. It was anti-idolatry of wealth.

---

## 4. Knowledge as sacred responsibility

**Claim:** The Prophetic age planted a knowledge civilization.

**Evidence grade:** B.

**Proof spine:** Revelation, recitation, memorization, teaching, transmission, adab, and community learning.

**Caveat:** Later science also depended on many external and institutional factors.

**Safe wording:** The Prophetic age made knowledge, memory, teaching, and transmission central to religious life.

**Unsafe wording:** Later science came only from early religious teaching.

**Content angle:** The libraries began with knowledge becoming sacred.

---

## 5. Mercy as architecture

**Claim:** The Prophetic age turned mercy into social architecture.

**Evidence grade:** B.

**Proof spine:** Care for the vulnerable, neighbor ethics, family duties, charity, restraint, and public responsibility.

**Caveat:** Later societies did not always live up to the seed.

**Safe wording:** Mercy can be studied not only as personal virtue but as a principle that shaped social obligations.

**Unsafe wording:** Every early or later practice was uniformly merciful.

**Content angle:** A civilization is judged by what it protects.
